about

A collaborator, technologist and artist based in Toronto.

Enthusiastic about knowledge-sharing, accessible design practices and maker cultures. Knowledgeable with digital fabrication tools, 2D design and motion compositions, tangible electronic media and generative code-based art.

My art explores the hierarchical relationships present in transformations of matter into form. I have found a strong interest in building interactive kinetic sculptures in addition to algorithmically-generated, screen-based work. I create mirrors through which I am able to better understand and meaningfully respond to the essence of relational hierarchies.

artwork

"love; the whole, together with &separately from, the parts." is a collaboration with Anneka Muther.

We are bodies composed of forces that repel and attract. Forces of attraction allow us to build connections and braid understanding. Forces of repulsion encourage categorization and definition. Despite this distinction, characteristics that are used to differentiate beings are the same factors that unify the beings. The bodies that the forces share choose to highlight division. The decision to highlight unique characteristics consequently amplifies individuality.

Both the forces of attraction and repulsion are needed to work in tandem to resolve their imbalance. Neither force is more valuable or negligible than the other. Both forces are parts of the whole. By moving down the road together, the forces of attraction and repulsion reframe what already is.

March 2020.

Our lives and everything in the universe can be approached with two different lenses: that of specification or generalization. Colonialism imports with it a system of differentiation; colonial enframing creates an intellectual divide as a self-other dualism between “society” and “nature”.

"Tapestry of Healing" is a collaboration with Anneka Muther. The physical structure of the project features a round-shaped planter supporting soil, plantlife, and woven material. The circular base embodies notions of infinity, revolution, inclusion, and nurture. Unravelled material from found discarded textiles are woven amongst plant growth. The vegetation has the choice either supporting or rejecting the imposed woven structure.

April 2019.

What is mathematics? A description of the world and things that occur within it? Has humanity uncovered the underlying property of the universe? Or has humanity merely invented it?

"Pi Playground" is a data visualization performance piece that explores the above questions. Using the first million digits of pi, Anitha Nathan and I collaborated to liberate pi from the constraining bounds of numerical symbolism.

We did this by translating the decimal digits to hexadecimal digits, and converted these translated values into RGB light through serial communication from Processing to Arduino. The values were additionally transformed into sound, allowing an audience to perceive pi from a different perspective. Overall, the experience of pi becomes more encircling and apart of our physicality.

October 2018.

To sit in a chair is to deprive the body of positional agency, enforcing conformation and disconnection with the sitter's body, others and environment.

"To Remind" is a chair-like object whose affordances can only be accessed through human communication and action. This project invites participants to recognize the isolating effects of single-user objects. This project critiques how object architectures serve the environment and the community in the long term. I aimed to redesign chairs to unite people and encourage reconnections with what we have lost touch.

May 2018.

"Mind Set" is a video art peice that approaches the subject of computation as being modelled after cognitive mapping processes. The brain's ability to compress information into concepts is a necessary condition for human existence.

Videos are frames of two-dimensional images. Rather than refreshing the image in its entirety every frame, individual frames are compressed in relation to the frames preceding and following them. This interframe codec overlays full reference frames with partial predicted frames. This method compresses two-dimensional data in three-dimensions, behaving in a way that simulates human cognition.

In this sense, "Mind Set" aims to explore the origins of computation.

December 2017.
